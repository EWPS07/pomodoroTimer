About the technique

For so many people, including myself, keeping focused on tasks for extended periods of time can prove to be quite difficult. Life offers up so many distractions throughout the day, from phone calls to emails, to social media. Everything has it's time and place, but keeping everything in order and in it's proper time slot can be an uphill battle.


Many people have found that the Pomodoro Technique can be incredibly useful to help them stay focused. Many people have less difficulty while focusing on tasks for short bursts, with quick breaks in between to break up the monotony, and that's where the Pomodoro Technique comes in.


The original Pomodoro is 4 rounds. The first 3 rounds each consist of a 25 minute work slot, and a 5 minute break. After the fourth work slot, a 10 minute break is given. In total, one full pomodoro round is 100 minutes of work, and 25 minutes for breaks(email, phone calls, social media, grab a coffee, or whatever you like).


Studies have shown that using the Pomodoro Technique or other methods similar to it, can drastically improve the amount of work one completes. The original time restraints aren't for everyone. Some people needs a break every half hour, so I've included a 50/10/20 option, the 52/17 method (not a pomodoro, but another method to boost productivity), and the ability to customize your work and break times so that you can find out what works best for you. Good luck!
